Book Ban

Books are a powerful thing. They have the ability to help us escape into another world or help you learn more about your own. They are a portal to different life experiences, and reading encourages empathy and social-emotional development. You pick up a fantasy book, you escape into another world or you pick up a book that tells you about the history of a place, you learn more about the place you are reading about

Either way books help you increase your knowledge, be it a fantasy world where your imagination is expanded or a historical book, you always learn. Books are oftentimes banned for multiple reasons, such as ‘explicit language’, ‘racist language’ etc. For example, J D Salinger’s The Catcher in The Rye which was banned for its ‘excessive vulgar language’. Harper Lee’s To Kill a Mockingbird was banned because the characters used racist language. Whereas George Orwell’s 1984 was banned for its social political theme. James Joyce’s Ulysses which was initially banned for its obscene language once is now a celebrated form of literature. There is a pattern to be noticed here where most of the books here were banned because of their themes and the languages used in them. But these books ended up being literary classics that are still taught in modern classrooms.

The Indian Education system has had a track record of producing malfunctioning robots with no skills. To sustain in this competitive world, a person must have cognitive, technical, social, and behavioral skills. Unfortunately, our Indian education system doesn't focus on any of that. It is busy finishing the syllabus and encourages rote learning. Students focus on Labs, externals, internals, viva, assignments, and final marks. If a student wastes his time by memorizing answers, doing pointless experiments, or reading just for examination, when will he develop his skills?? When will he do something productive?? When will he learn about new opportunities?? When will he focus on his career development???

Over 1000 books have been banned worldwide, this number includes books which are old and new alike.

Few of the new books that have been banned are The Bluest Eyes by Toni Morrison, her debut novel, which talks about the horrors of racial oppression women have to go through, it also talks about the tyranny and the violation brought upon women by men in their lives. One such example of book ban is about a book called Maus by Art Spiegelman which is a graphic novel that talks about the experiences off his father as a Polish Jew and Holocaust survivor. Maus is a story that consists of two books, Book One: My Father Bleeds and Book Two: And Here My Troubles Began. The series famously articulates its characters as animals; they understand themselves as human, but readers see Jews as mice, Nazis as cats, Polish gentiles as pigs, and Americans as dogs. The drawing allows Spiegelman to do more than say what happened. In a rich, layered way, he can show it. Importance of books like Maus cannot be overstated: It shifted how people talk about history, trauma, and ethnic and racial persecution.
Maus being a very powerful read is also a tricky text, prone to misinterpretation. Wikipedia defines book censorship as the act of some authority taking measures to suppress ideas and information within a book. One study found that reading J.K. Rowling’s Harry Potter series, which is frequently challenged for religious concerns about witchcraft, “improved attitudes” about immigrants, homosexuals, and refugees. Another study found that reading narrative fiction helped readers understand their peers and raised social abilities.

So, in a world were free speech is celebrated and encouraged is it justified that the books are censored?
